Lumpia

Where do Lumpias come from?

Lumpias are a fried spring roll not to be mistaken by a chinese egg roll, it's origins come from the Philippines and parts of Indonesia. The roll is typically deepfried in a thin wrapper and stuffed with pork often mixed in with vegetables like carrot,onions, and green onion!

Ingredients needed to make Lumpia

  1. 1lb of ground pork
  2. onion,garlic,carrot, and green onion
  3. lumpia wrappers
  4. Vegetable oil for frying

How to make Lumpia

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add pork; cook and stir until crumbly and no pink is showing, 5 to 7 minutes. Remove pork from the pan and set aside. Drain grease from the pan, leaving just a thin coating.
  2. Add onion and garlic to the pan; cook and stir until fragrant, about 2 minutes. Stir in the cooked pork, carrots, green onions, cabbage, and cilantro. Season with pepper, salt, garlic powder, and soy sauce. Remove from the heat, and set aside until cool enough to handle, about 5 minutes.
  3. Lay out the lumpia wrapper and mix in the pork and vegetables together until thoroughly mixed together. Begin to wrap the lumpia paper from one corner to the other.
  4. Fill a dutch oven with oil enough to shallow fry the lumpia. Leave the lumpia in for about 5 minutes or golden brown.
  5. Serve with a sweet and sour sauce, enjoy!
